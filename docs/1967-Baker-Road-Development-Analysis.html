## R-4A ZONING REQUIREMENTS


### Overview of R-4A Zone


R-4A is a **single-family residential district** designed for medium-density residential development in Atlanta. It's commonly found in established neighborhoods with traditional lot patterns.


### Permitted Uses


**Principal Uses:**

- ✅ Single-family detached dwellings (PRIMARY USE)

- ✅ Public schools (through secondary level, operated by Atlanta Board of Education)

- ✅ MARTA structures and uses (excluding storage/maintenance facilities)

- ❌ Duplexes/two-family dwellings NOT permitted (require R-5 zoning)

- ❌ Townhomes NOT permitted

- ❌ Multi-family NOT permitted


**Accessory Uses:**

- ✅ Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) - Maximum 750 sq ft, 20 ft height

- ✅ Guest houses (subject to lot coverage limits)

- ✅ Home occupations

- ✅ Customary residential accessory structures (garages, sheds, etc.)

#### Geotechnical / Soil Testing


**Purpose:** Determine soil bearing capacity and foundation requirements


**When Required:**

- **REQUIRED** for building permit approval in most jurisdictions

- Needed for structural engineer to design foundations

- Critical for understanding construction costs


**Atlanta Soil Conditions:**

- Weathered crystalline rock ("saprolite") common

- Variable bearing capacity (can range from excellent to poor)

- May require deeper footings or soil stabilization in some areas

- Expansive clay soils possible (can cause foundation movement)


**Testing Process:**

- Soil borings at multiple locations across site

- Laboratory analysis of soil samples

- Bearing capacity determination

- Recommendations for foundation type and depth


**Cost:** $2,000-$5,000 per site (may test 2-3 representative lots for subdivision)

**Timeline:** 7-14 days

**Recommendation:** ORDER THIS DURING DUE DILIGENCE - results could significantly impact costs


**Potential Findings:**

- Good bearing capacity (2,000+ psf): Standard shallow footings, lower cost

- Poor bearing capacity (<1,000 psf): Deeper footings, engineered fill, higher cost

- Rock near surface: Blasting required (expensive: $10,000-$50,000+)

- High water table: Special drainage, potential foundation issues

**Purpose:** Show existing ground elevations for grading and drainage design


**When Required:**

- Needed for civil engineering (street, drainage design)

- Required for land disturbance permit

- Used to calculate cut/fill volumes and costs


**Deliverable:** CAD drawing with contour lines (typically 1-ft or 2-ft intervals)


**Cost:** $3,000-$5,000 for 1.6-acre site
