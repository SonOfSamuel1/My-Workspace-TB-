## R-4A ZONING REQUIREMENTS

### Overview of R-4A Zone

R-4A is a **single-family residential district** designed for medium-density residential development in Atlanta. It's commonly found in established neighborhoods with traditional lot patterns.

### Permitted Uses

**Principal Uses:**
- ✅ Single-family detached dwellings (PRIMARY USE)
- ✅ Public schools (through secondary level, operated by Atlanta Board of Education)
- ✅ MARTA structures and uses (excluding storage/maintenance facilities)
- ❌ Duplexes/two-family dwellings NOT permitted (require R-5 zoning)
- ❌ Townhomes NOT permitted
- ❌ Multi-family NOT permitted

**Accessory Uses:**
- ✅ Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) - Maximum 750 sq ft, 20 ft height
- ✅ Guest houses (subject to lot coverage limits)
- ✅ Home occupations
- ✅ Customary residential accessory structures (garages, sheds, etc.)

### Minimum Lot Requirements (Per §16-06A.007)

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| **Minimum Lot Area** | 7,500 sq ft |
| **Minimum Lot Width/Frontage** | 50 feet |
| **Special Note** | Lots under 7,500 sq ft have different FAR (65% vs 50%) |

**Application to Your Property:**
- Total area: 70,527 sq ft
- Theoretical maximum lots: 70,527 ÷ 7,500 = **9.4 lots**
- Realistic capacity: **8-10 lots** (accounting for street, setbacks, utilities)

### Setback Requirements (Per §16-06A.008)

| Setback Type | Requirement | Notes |
|--------------|-------------|-------|
| **Front Yard** | 35 feet | Some sources indicate 20 feet - verify with City Planning |
| **Side Yards** | 7 feet (each side) | Total of 14 feet across property |
| **Rear Yard** | 15 feet | From rear property line |

For a 50-ft wide × 150-ft deep lot (7,500 sq ft):

## BUILDING & PERMIT REQUIREMENTS

### Permit Process (City of Atlanta / Fulton County)

#### Required Permits & Approvals

1. **Land Disturbance Permit**
   - Required if: >1 acre of land disturbed
   - Issued by: City of Atlanta Department of Watershed Management
   - Timeline: 30-60 days
   - Requirements: Erosion control plan, stormwater management plan

2. **Subdivision Plat Approval**
   - Required for: Creating new lots from single parcel
   - Governed by: Atlanta Land Subdivision Ordinance (Part 15)
   - Process:
     - Pre-application meeting with City Planning
     - Submit preliminary plat
     - City review (60-90 days)
     - Address comments, revise
     - Final plat approval
     - Record plat in Fulton County Superior Court
   - Timeline: 6-12 months total
   - Contact: Atlanta Dept. of City Planning (404-330-6145)

3. **Building Permits** (per dwelling)
   - Required for: Each house and ADU
   - Issued by: City of Atlanta or Fulton County (verify jurisdiction)
   - Requirements:
     - Architectural drawings
     - Structural calculations
     - Energy compliance (IECC)
     - Plumbing, mechanical, electrical plans
     - Soil report
   - Timeline: 30-45 days per permit
   - Fees: Approximately $7 per $1,000 of construction value

4. **Floodplain Development Permit**
   - Required for: Properties in FEMA flood zones
   - **Status for Your Property:** NOT REQUIRED (property not in flood zone)
   - This saves time and money in permitting process

### Environmental & Site Assessments

#### Phase I Environmental Site Assessment

**Purpose:** Identify potential environmental contamination or liabilities

**When Required:**

- Protects buyer from inheriting environmental liabilities

**Process:**

**Cost:** $1,700-$2,700 for simple vacant lot

**Recommendation:** ORDER THIS DURING DUE DILIGENCE PERIOD

**Potential Issues to Watch For:**
- Former gas station or automotive use nearby
- Industrial operations in area history
- Underground storage tanks
- Dumping or fill material
- Wetlands or protected species habitat

#### Geotechnical / Soil Testing

**Purpose:** Determine soil bearing capacity and foundation requirements

**When Required:**
- **REQUIRED** for building permit approval in most jurisdictions
- Needed for structural engineer to design foundations
- Critical for understanding construction costs

**Atlanta Soil Conditions:**
- Weathered crystalline rock ("saprolite") common
- Variable bearing capacity (can range from excellent to poor)
- May require deeper footings or soil stabilization in some areas
- Expansive clay soils possible (can cause foundation movement)

**Testing Process:**
- Soil borings at multiple locations across site
- Laboratory analysis of soil samples
- Bearing capacity determination
- Recommendations for foundation type and depth

**Cost:** $2,000-$5,000 per site (may test 2-3 representative lots for subdivision)
**Timeline:** 7-14 days
**Recommendation:** ORDER THIS DURING DUE DILIGENCE - results could significantly impact costs

**Potential Findings:**
- Good bearing capacity (2,000+ psf): Standard shallow footings, lower cost
- Poor bearing capacity (<1,000 psf): Deeper footings, engineered fill, higher cost
- Rock near surface: Blasting required (expensive: $10,000-$50,000+)
- High water table: Special drainage, potential foundation issues

#### Topographic Survey

**Purpose:** Show existing ground elevations for grading and drainage design

**When Required:**
- Needed for civil engineering (street, drainage design)
- Required for land disturbance permit
- Used to calculate cut/fill volumes and costs

**Deliverable:** CAD drawing with contour lines (typically 1-ft or 2-ft intervals)

**Cost:** $3,000-$5,000 for 1.6-acre site
**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks
**Note:** May already exist from 2018 survey; verify with surveyor if elevations included

### Stormwater Management

#### City of Atlanta Stormwater Requirements

**Triggered By:**
- Any new home or addition >1,000 sq ft of impervious surface
- Land disturbance >1 acre

**Requirements:**
1. **Stormwater Management Plan**
   - Must be prepared by licensed professional engineer
   - Design per Georgia Stormwater Management Manual
   - Show pre- and post-development runoff
   - Demonstrate no net increase in peak runoff

2. **Best Management Practices (BMPs)**
   - Detention/retention ponds (most common for subdivisions)
   - Bioretention areas
   - Permeable pavement
   - Underground detention systems

3. **Inspection and Maintenance Agreement**
   - Required for all stormwater facilities
   - Agreement executed with City of Atlanta
   - **Recorded in Fulton County Superior Court deed records**
   - Creates perpetual maintenance obligation for property owner
   - Includes inspection schedule and reporting requirements

**Design Considerations:**
- Atlanta's hilly terrain creates significant runoff
- Detention ponds typically require 5-10% of total site area
- Must maintain 100-year flood control
- Outfall location and capacity critical

**Cost:** $50,000-$150,000 for detention pond serving 9-lot subdivision (estimate)
