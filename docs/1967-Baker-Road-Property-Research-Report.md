## 10. CONCLUSION

The property at 1967 Baker Road NW presents a complex investment opportunity in a neighborhood undergoing significant transition. Grove Park benefits from major infrastructure investment, strong regional economic fundamentals, and documented Beltline-related appreciation potential. However, current market softness, above-average crime rates, and a significant price gap between target development pricing ($650K-$900K) and current market ceiling ($500K-$530K) present substantial risks.

**Key Decision Factors:**

1. **Can the market support $650K-$900K pricing?** Current data suggests this would be 20-70% above proven price points. Success would require exceptional quality, unique features, or targeting relocators from higher-cost markets.

2. **Is market timing favorable?** Current market conditions (declining prices, extended DOM, low competition) suggest waiting for stabilization or planning for extended sales periods.

3. **Will Beltline appreciation materialize?** Historical data shows 17.9-26.6% additional appreciation within 0.5 mile of Beltline. However, timing of realization is uncertain.

4. **Can crime concerns be overcome?** F-grade for violent crime and property crime rates may limit buyer pool willing to pay premium prices.

**Recommendation:** Proceed with comprehensive parcel-specific due diligence, but maintain conservative assumptions about achievable pricing. Consider whether a more modest price point ($500K-$600K range) might offer better risk-adjusted returns. Engage local real estate professionals with specific Grove Park new construction experience before committing to development plans.

The opportunity exists, but success requires realistic pricing expectations, exceptional execution, patient capital, and likely a longer-term investment horizon than initially anticipated.
